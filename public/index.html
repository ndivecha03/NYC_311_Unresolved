The Problem

Complaints don't just sit. They get lost — sometimes for years.

NYC DOT maintains 250,000 streetlamps. When one fails, residents call 311 and the complaint enters a queue that, in Manhattan, closes within thirty days only 67% of the time. Citywide, that figure is climbing — total 311 streetlight reports rose from 30,881 in 2024 to 33,835 in 2025. The remaining third get duplicated, merged, or administratively shut without the lamp ever turning back on.

Meanwhile, 1,242 licensed local electricians live in the same neighborhoods — qualified, idle, and shut out of the pipeline. We connect the two.
